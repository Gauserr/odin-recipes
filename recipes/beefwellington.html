Beef Wellington

Description

Beef Wellington is a steak dish popularized in England. The aesthetically stunning dish is comprised of a seared and dijon-mustard brushed fillet that is wrapped in a mushroom Duxelle , parma ham, a puff-pastry, and brushed with eggwash. The dish jumped in popularity after being praised and perfected by infamous chef Gordon Ramsey. While this recipe guides you through creating the main entree, the dish is often supplemented with mashed potatoes, steamed carrots, and optionally a red wine sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 10-12 oz. fillet
  • 1 cup portabello mushrooms
  • 2 springs of Thyme
  • 1 tablesoon dijon-mustard
  • 1 puff-pastry
  • salt
  • pepper
  • 1 egg
  • 6-8 slices parma ham

Steps

  1. Let fillet sit at room temp for 10 minutes.
  2. Sear each side of the fillet for 30 seconds on med-high heat.
  3. Immeidately brush the entire surface of the fillet with dijon mustard
  4. Very finely dice all of your mushrooms, and place in a pan over low heat for 45 minutes to an hour, or until all moisture is removed.
  5. Sprinkle springs of Thyme over the mushrooms.
  6. On a strip of cling film, lay out your parma ham in an interlocking method from right left, forming a solid layer of ham.
  7. Spread your mushroom duxelle evenly across the entire surface of the ham.
  8. Set your fillet on top of the mushrooms, and use the cling film to tightly wrap the fillet.
  9. Refrigerate the fillet for 45 minutes.
  10. Remove the cling film from the fillet, and on another piece of film lay out your puff-pastry
  11. Set the fillet on the puff-pastry, and once again use the cling film to wrap the fillet.
  12. Regrigerate for another 45 minutes.
  13. Preheat oven to 425 degrees Fahrenheit.
  14. In a bowl, whisk one egg yolk and a dash of water. Brush the mixture over the entire surface of the pastry.
  15. Bake for ~25-30 minutes or until it reaches an internal temp of ~130 degrees Fahrenheit.
